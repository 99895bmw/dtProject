Major Crops of India & Modern Practices

Understanding the specific needs of different crops is crucial for successful agriculture. Here's information on some key Indian crops and how modern techniques can enhance their cultivation.

Rice (Paddy)

A staple food crop. Modern practices include System of Rice Intensification (SRI), direct seeding, and use of high-yielding, disease-resistant varieties. Water management is critical. Consider drip irrigation for upland rice.

Wheat

A major rabi crop. Zero tillage farming, bed planting, and use of certified seeds improve yield. Timely irrigation and balanced fertilization are key. Monitor for rust diseases.

Millets (Jowar, Bajra, Ragi)

Drought-tolerant and nutritious. Promote intercropping, use of improved varieties, and efficient post-harvest processing. Growing demand for their health benefits.

Sugarcane

Important cash crop. Sustainable practices include trash mulching, drip irrigation (saving water and improving yield), and integrated pest management for borers and other pests.

Cotton

Key fiber crop. Focus on high-density planting, IPM for pests like pink bollworm, and water-use efficiency. Bt cotton varieties are common but require careful management.

Chickpea (Chana)

Major pulse crop (Rabi). Use of rhizobium biofertilizer, resistant varieties for wilt and blight, and proper spacing can significantly boost yields. Important for soil health due to nitrogen fixation.
